Jacqueline Casey

Life Before Graphic Design

Even as a child she wanted to be an artist, but her parents did not support this decision. Even so she trained at Massachusetts College of Art and earned her bachelors in a fine arts degree concentrating in fashion design and illustration.

Awards and Honors

Casey has received numerous awards and honors for her work

About Jacqueline Casey

Jacqueline Casey working in her studio.

After college she had a hard time finding a job and she decided to travel to Europe. When she returned she was ready to return to the arts and focus on design. She worked in jobs for interior design and advertising before getting hired at MIT in 1955. At MIT she joined the Office of Publications which is now known as the Design Services Office. When Jacqueline first joined this team Muriel Cooper was the director of the department and the one who recruited Jacqueline. When she left MIT Jacqueline became the new department director in 1972. During this time there were not many women working at a professional level and holding high positions. Most of their colleagues were male but they were still able to work together to create amazing designs. Cooper described Jacqueline as “The spirit of MIT nurtured her work, and in turn, her work nurtured the humanity of MIT.” Jacqueline Casey’s work is influenced by Swiss designers. She once said, “My job is to stop anyone I can with an arresting or puzzling image, and entice the viewer to read the message in small type and above all to attend the exhibition.” She made many posters for MIT to publicize their exhibitions, many say her designs at MIT are what she's defined by.
